Digital Meal Kit Instructions

Digital meal kit assembly instructions that I designed and wrote the logic for using the manufacturing app platform Tulip. This design went through several iterations and testing phases, and it was when I learned that UX design was my passion. Every aspect of the design from the language of the instructions, to the font size, to the actions that could be performed at each stage of the process, had to be well thought out with a specific end user in mind. I loved coming up with creative ways to solve potential problems our user might have.

Just to illustrate what these screenshots are showing and tell the story of the design, the process had two parts to it: an ingredient prepping phase to be done at the beginning of the workday and a box packing phase for when an order was placed. As shown by these screenshots, with well researched design choices, clear labels, and just a small amount of training, our user was able to learn on the fly where in the store to find each and every ingredient, how exactly to prepare the ingredient with both written instructions and videos, how much to pack in each bag, and even which bag to pack it in. The process was cleverly designed so that the user couldn’t move on to the next step without properly inputting that a bag was packed, and even further, that input wouldn’t be registered unless the weight on the connected scale was within an acceptable range. Everything was kept track of on our back end which I helped design with our developer, so that no orders were missed, so that any bottlenecks in the process could be highlighted and corrected, and so that everybody involved could have a positive user experience without any stress or headaches.

Digital Meal Kit User Experience in Action

A video that I shot and edited showing our company’s meal kit station in action at one of our grocery clients’ locations. With just a few materials, a laptop, and a tablet, the user (no matter their level of familiarity with either food prep or technology) was able to quickly and seamlessly put together as many as 50 made-to-order meal kit boxes per day in less than 10 minutes per box due to the well designed, foolproof process. The testimonials we received from both employees and customers were highly helpful as a form of user research and they proved how a positive experience for our end user (the grocery store itself) translates to a positive experience for their end user (their customers) as well, thus ensuring a mutually beneficial partnership.
